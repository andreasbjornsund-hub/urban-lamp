What makes a smartphone "Zone 1 certified"?

Zone 1 is the second most hazardous explosive atmosphere classification — defined by IEC 60079-10 as an area where an explosive gas atmosphere is likely to occur during normal operation. Think pump rooms on chemical tankers, gas processing areas in refineries, or classified zones in LNG terminals.

A device certified for Zone 1 must meet specific protection concepts under the ATEX or IECEx standard. For portable electronics, this almost always means intrinsic safety (Ex ia or Ex ib) — the device is engineered so that it cannot release enough energy (electrical or thermal) to ignite a surrounding explosive atmosphere, even in fault conditions.

Not all "rugged" phones are Zone 1 certified. Military-spec ruggedness (MIL-STD-810) and IP68 water resistance have nothing to do with Zone 1 certification. A phone can be fully waterproof and survive a 1.5m drop but still be prohibited in your gas processing area.
